Different Styles of Dance

Style Descripton Needed Equipment
Acro Acro is a style that combines technical training with acrobatic elements to show off different styles of flexibility. This style of dance is a lot newer and became widespread in the later twentieth century. Acro is highly defined by it's althetic character which blends with it's unique elemants and coreography of other dance technique. Barefeet, moveable clothing, and hair out of the face.
Ballet Ballet is a highly technical style of dance which requires much training. Ballet is the first official style of dance which orginated during the italian Renaissance in the fifteenth century, then spread towards France and Russia. Since then, it has become highly widespread, with it's own terminology based off of the french language. This tryle of dance is hghly technical, yet fluid, and it's an overall gracefull art with lots of work. Ballet shoes, leotard, tights, and a proper ballet bun.
Contemporary Contemporary is a highly unique style of dance which allows dancers to explore body movement and create new boundaries. This style of dance was developed during the mid-twentieth century, and is now one of the more dominant genres for formally trained dancers. Contemporary is a highly technical genre which also allows dancers to break free from traditional training and create new ways of moving and body awareness. Turn shoes/fundies or socks, moveable clothing, and hair out of the face.
Hip Hop Hip hop is considered more of a casual and street style of dance. This style of dance was developed in the 1970s and became popular throughout dance crews. Now hip hop is one of the most common styles of dance, as it's a great way to "let loose" and have some fun. Also, hip hop is very active and all about coordination. This style of dance is mainly focused on high energy and facial expressions. Clean runners/sneakers, moveable clothing, and hair out of the face.
Jazz Jazz is a style that is made to show off technical skills with a more stylish or sasssy performance aspect. This style of dance was developed and became widespread in the early twentieth century. Jazz is a very standard dance as in it creates a solid performance and technical base for other dances. Also, it's a very powerful style as there are no boundaries restricting dancers' technical aspects. Jazz shoes/fundies, moveable clothing, and hair out of the face.
Lyrical Lyrical is a style quite similar to Jazz however, dancers' technique is shown with a more graceful performance as opposed to powerful like Jazz. This style of dance was developed within the first half of the twentieth century. Lyrical is also a dance that combines many aspects from other dances like ballet, jazz, acro, and newer modern movements. Fundies, moveable clothing, and hair out of the face.
Musical Theatre Musical Theatre is a style that combines dance with song, so while the dancers perform, they also sing. THe idea of Musical Theatre was developed in the fifth century in Greece. Now, Musical Thetre is most commonly associated with Broadway as they are the same thing just in different depths. This style is definately mainly focused on performing skills and entertainment, so it's one of the most fun styles. Jazz shoes, moveable clothing, singing voices, and hair out of the face
Tap Tap is a very different and unique style of dance as it is mainly focused on footwork and making music with steps. This tyle of dance was developed in the nineteenth century. Tap is a cross between African and Irsh dance forms by combining Irish fottwork with African percussion. Eventually, the style evolved to what it is. Now, tap is a fun and happy dance style with fun arm movements and music to match their rythmic footwork. Tap shoes, moveable clothing, and hair out of the face.

There are numerous amounts of other dance styles. However the ones on this chart are the most modern and common styles nowadays.
